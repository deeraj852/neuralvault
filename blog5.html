How to Use Midjourney for Beginners 2026

NeuralVault EditorialMarch 15, 20269 min read
Midjourney is the most powerful AI image generator available today — but getting started can feel overwhelming. This complete beginner's guide walks you through everything from account setup to writing prompts that produce stunning results every single time. By the end of this article you will be creating professional-quality AI artwork confidently.

What is Midjourney?

Midjourney is an AI image generation tool that creates stunning visuals from text descriptions called prompts. You describe what you want to see, and Midjourney produces four image options within about a minute. The quality is consistently remarkable — producing everything from photorealistic portraits to abstract art, architectural concepts, and product mockups.

Unlike some competitors, Midjourney runs through Discord, the popular messaging platform. This makes it slightly different from typical web apps, but once you understand the workflow it becomes second nature.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Step 01
Create a Discord Account
Go to discord.com and sign up for a free account. You need this to access Midjourney. The process takes about two minutes — just enter your email, create a username, and verify your account.
Step 02
Join the Midjourney Server
Go to midjourney.com and click "Join the Beta". This takes you directly to the official Midjourney Discord server. Accept the invite and you are in. Browse the channels to get a feel for what others are creating.
Step 03
Subscribe to a Plan
Midjourney requires a paid subscription starting at $10 per month. Go to midjourney.com/account and choose your plan. The Basic plan at $10/mo gives you 200 image generations per month — more than enough to learn and experiment.
Step 04
Find a Newbies Channel
Inside the Midjourney Discord server, look for channels named "newbies-1", "newbies-2", etc. in the left sidebar. These are where beginners generate images. Click on any newbies channel to start.
Step 05
Write Your First Prompt
In the message box at the bottom, type /imagine followed by a space and your description. For example: /imagine a golden retriever puppy sitting in a field of sunflowers, warm afternoon light, photorealistic. Press Enter and wait about 60 seconds for your images.

How to Write Great Prompts

The quality of your results depends almost entirely on the quality of your prompt. A well-written prompt consistently produces stunning images while a vague prompt produces mediocre ones.

Basic Prompt Structure

A good Midjourney prompt follows this structure:

Prompt Formula
[Subject] + [Setting/Environment] + [Style] + [Lighting] + [Camera/Mood]

Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid

  • Prompts too short — Give Midjourney detail to work with. Short prompts produce generic results.
  • Too many conflicting ideas — Focus on one clear concept per image. Mixing too many themes confuses the output.
  • Not using aspect ratio — Always add --ar 16:9 for landscape, --ar 9:16 for portrait, or --ar 1:1 for square.
  • Ignoring variations — When you get a result you like, always click V1-V4 to explore variations before moving on.
  • Not saving good prompts — Keep a personal collection of prompts that work well. They save enormous time later.

🎯 You Are Ready to Start

Midjourney has one of the steepest perceived learning curves of any AI tool — but in reality it becomes intuitive very quickly. Start with simple prompts, experiment with style keywords, and learn from what works. Within a week of regular practice you will be producing images that genuinely impress people.
